Zinaida Yermolyeva

a microbiologist, bacteriochemist, and cholera expert of the Soviet era, who saved millions and invented a type of penicillin in field operation during WWII

Here's a timeline of Zinaida Yermolyeva's life:

  • October 24, 1898 - was born in Frolovo, Russia
  • 1942 - Obtained the first Soviet samples of penicillin
  • 1947 - Obtained laboratory samples of streptomycin
  • Developed a number of Soviet antibiotic agents including interferon, ekmonovicillin, Bicillins, ekmolin, and dipasfen
  • Received the highest scientific honors the Soviet Union bestowed
  • 1974 - died
